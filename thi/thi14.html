Therigatha - The Verses of the Elder Nuns

Chapter 14: The Group of (about) Thirty Verses

  • Translated from the Pali by Mrs. Rhys Davids
Subhājīvakambavanikā

368In Jīvaka’s pleasant woodland walked Subhā
The Bhikkhunī. A gallant met her there
And barred the way. To him thus spake Subhā:

369‘What have I done to offend thee, that thus in my path thou comest?
No man, O friend, it beseemeth to touch a Sister in Orders.

370So hath my Master ordained in the precepts we honour and follow;
So hath the Welcome One taught in the training wherein they have trained me,
Purified discipline holy. Why standest thou blocking my pathway?

371Me pure, thou impure of heart; me passionless, thou of vile passions;
Me who as to the whole of me freed am in spirit and blameless,
Me whence comes it that Thou dost hinder, standing obnoxious?’

372‘Young art thou, maiden, and faultless — what seekest thou in the holy life?
Cast off that yellow-hued raiment and come! in the blossoming woodland

373Seek we our pleasure. Filled with the incense of blossoms the trees waft
Sweetness. See, the spring’s at the prime, the season of happiness!
Come with me then to the flowering woodland, and seek we our pleasure.

374Sweet overhead is the sough of the blossoming crests of the forest
Swayed by the Wind-gods. But an thou goest alone in the jungle,

375Lost in its depths, how wilt thou find aught to delight or content thee?
Haunted is the great forest with many a herd of wild creatures,
Broken its peace by the tramplings of elephants rutting and savage.

376Empty of mankind and fearsome — is’t there thou would’st go uncompanioned?
Thou like a gold-wrought statue, like nymph in celestial garden
Movest, O peerless creature. Radiant would shine thy loveliness
Robed in raiment of beauty, diaphanous gear of Benares.

377I would live but to serve thee, an thou would’st abide in the woodland.
Dearer and sweeter to me than art thou in the world is no creature,
Thou with the languid and slow-moving eyes of an elf of the forest.

378If thou wilt list to me, come where the joys of the sheltered life wait thee:
Dwell in a house of verandas and terraces, handmaidens serving thee.

379Robe thyself in delicate gear of Benares, don garlands, use unguents.
Ornaments many and divers I give to thee, fashioned with precious stones,
Gold work and pearls. And thou shalt mount on a couch fair and sumptuous,

380Carvèd in sandalwood, fragrant with essences, spread with new pillows,
Coverlets fleecy and soft, and decked with immaculate canopies.

381Like to a lotus upborne on the bosom of sprite-haunted water,
Thou, O chaste anchorite, farest to old age, thy beauty unmated.’

382‘What now to thee, in this carrion-filled, grave-filling carcass so fragile
Seen by thee, seemeth to warrant the doctrine thou speakest, infatuate?’

383‘Eyes hast thou like the gazelle’s, like an elf’s in the heart of the mountains —
’Tis those eyes of thee, sight of which feedeth the depth of my passion.

384Shrined in thy dazzling, immaculate face as in calyx of lotus,
’Tis those eyes of thee, sight of which feedeth the strength of my passion.

385Though thou be far from me, how could I ever forget thee, O maiden,
Thee of the long-drawn eyelashes, thee of the eyes so miraculous?

386Dearer to me than those orbs is naught, O thou witching-eyed fairy!’
‘Lo! thou art wanting to walk where no path is; thou seekest to capture
Moon from the skies for thy play; thou would’st jump o’er the ridges of Meru,
Thou who presumest to lie in wait for a child of the Buddha!

387Nowhere in earth or in heaven lives now any object of lust for me.
Him I know not. What like is he? Slain, root and branch, through the Noble Path.

388Hurled as live coal from the hand, and rated as deadly as poison-cup,
Him I see not. What like is he? Slain, root and branch, through the Noble Path.

389Tempt thou some woman who hath not discerned what I say, or whose teacher
Is but a learner; haply she’ll listen; tempt thou not Subhā;

390She understandeth. And now ’tis thyself hast vexation and failure.
For I have set my mind to be watchful in whatso befalls me —
Blame or honour, gladness or sorrow — and knowing the principle: —
‘Foul are all composite things,’ nowhere the mind of me clings to them.

391Yea, the disciple am I of the Welcome One; onward the march of me
Riding the Car of the Road that is Eightfold. Drawn are the arrows
Out of my wounds, and purged is my spirit of drugging Intoxicants.
So I am come to haunts that are Empty. There lies my pleasure.

392Oh! I have seen it — a puppet well painted, with new wooden spindles,
Cunningly fastened with strings and with pins, and diversely dancing.

393But if the strings and the pins be all drawn out and loosened and scattered,
So that the puppet be made non-existent and broken in pieces,
Which of the parts wilt thou choose and appoint for thy heart’s rest and solace?

394Such is the manner wherein persist these poor little bodies:
Take away members and attributes — nothing surviveth in any wise.
Nothing surviveth! Which dost thou choose for thy heart’s rest and solace?

395E’en as a fresco one sees drawn on a wall, painted in ochre,
[Giveth us naught of the true and the real, save in the seeming ;]

396Thou herein with vision perverted [canst not distinguish;
Judgest with] wisdom of average human, fallible, worthless.
O thou art blind! thou chasest a sham, deluded by puppet shows
Seen in the midst of the crowd; thou deemest of value and genuine

397Conjurer’s trickwork, trees all of gold that we see in our dreaming.
What is this eye but a little ball lodged in the fork of a hollow tree,
Bubble of film, anointed with tear-brine, exuding slime-drops,
Compost wrought in the shape of an eye of manifold aspects?’

398Forthwith the maiden so lovely tore out her eye and gave it him:
‘Here, then! take thou thine eye!’ Nor sinned she, her heart unobstructed.

399Straightway the lust in him ceasèd and he her pardon imploring:
‘O that thou mightest recover thy sight, thou maid pure and holy!

400Never again will I dare to offend thee after this fashion.
Sore hast thou smitten my sin; blazing flames have I clasped to my bosom;
Poisonous snake have I handled — but O! be thou heal’d and forgive me!’

401Freed from molesting, the Bhikkhunī went on her way to the Buddha,
Chief of th’ Awakened. There in his presence, seeing those features
Born of uttermost merit, straightway her sight was restored to her.

Subhājīvakambavanikātherīgāthā

368p_19Th2_452Jīvakambavanaṁ rammaṁ,
Gacchantiṁ bhikkhuniṁ subhaṁ;
Dhuttako sannivāresi,
Tamenaṁ abravī subhā.

369p_19Th2_453"Kiṁ te aparādhitaṁ mayā,
Yaṁ maṁ ovariyāna tiṭṭhasi;
Na hi pabbajitāya āvuso,
Puriso samphusanāya kappati.

370 p_19Th2_454Garuke mama satthusāsane,
Yā sikkhā sugatena desitā;
Parisuddhapadaṁ anangaṇaṁ,
Kiṁ maṁ ovariyāna tiṭṭhasi.

371p_19Th2_455Āvilacitto anāvilaṁ,
Sarajo vītarajaṁ anangaṇaṁ;
Sabbattha vimuttamānasaṁ,
Kiṁ maṁ ovariyāna tiṭṭhasi".

372p_19Th2_456"Daharā ca apāpikā casi,
Kiṁ te pabbajjā karissati;
Nikkhipa kāsāyacīvaraṁ,
Ehi ramāma supupphite vane.

373p_19Th2_457Madhurañca pavanti sabbaso,
Kusumarajena samuṭṭhitā dumā;
Paṭhamavasanto sukho utu,
Ehi ramāma supupphite vane.

374p_19Th2_458Kusumitasikharā ca pādapā,
Abhigajjantiva māluteritā;
Kā tuyhaṁ rati bhavissati,
Yadi ekā vanamogahissasi.

375p_19Th2_459Vāḷamigasanghasevitaṁ,
Kuñjaramattakareṇuloḷitaṁ;
Asahāyikā gantumicchasi,
Rahitaṁ bhiṁsanakaṁ mahāvanaṁ.

376p_19Th2_460Tapanīyakatāva dhītikā,
Vicarasi cittalateva accharā;
Kāsikasukhumehi vaggubhi,
Sobhasī suvasanehi nūpame.

377p_19Th2_461Ahaṁ tava vasānugo siyaṁ,
Yadi viharemase kānanantare;
Na hi matthi tayā piyattaro,
Pāṇo kinnarimandalocane.

378p_19Th2_462Yadi me vacanaṁ karissasi,
Sukhitā ehi agāramāvasa;
Pāsādanivātavāsinī,
Parikammaṁ te karontu nāriyo.

379 p_19Th2_463Kāsikasukhumāni dhāraya,
Abhiropehi ca mālavaṇṇakaṁ;
Kañcanamaṇimuttakaṁ bahuṁ,
Vividhaṁ ābharaṇaṁ karomi te.

380p_19Th2_464Sudhotarajapacchadaṁ subhaṁ,
Gonakatūlikasanthataṁ navaṁ;
Abhiruha sayanaṁ mahārahaṁ,
Candanamaṇḍitasāragandhikaṁ.

381p_19Th2_465Uppalaṁ cudakā samuggataṁ,
Yathā taṁ amanus­sasevitaṁ;
Evaṁ tvaṁ brahmacārinī,
Sakesangesu jaraṁ gamissasi".

382p_19Th2_466"Kiṁ te idha sārasammataṁ,
Kuṇapapūramhi susānavaḍḍhane;
Bhedanadhamme kaḷevare,
Yaṁ disvā vimano udikkhasi".

383p_19Th2_467"Akkhīni ca tūriyāriva,
Kinnariyāriva pabbatantare;
Tava me nayanāni dakkhiya,
Bhiyyo kāmaratī pavaḍḍhati.

384p_19Th2_468Uppalasikharopamāni te,
Vimale hāṭakasannibhe mukhe;
Tava me nayanāni dakkhiya,
Bhiyyo kāmaguṇo pavaḍḍhati.

385p_19Th2_469Api dūragatā saramhase,
Āyatapamhe visuddhadassane;
Na hi matthi tayā piyattaro,
Nayanā kinnarimandalocane".

386p_19Th2_470"Apathena payātumicchasi,
Candaṁ kīḷanakaṁ gavesasi;
Meruṁ langhetumicchasi,
Yo tvaṁ buddhasutaṁ maggayasi.

387 p_19Th2_471Natthi hi loke sadevake,
Rāgo yatthapi dāni me siyā;
Napi naṁ jānāmi kīriso,
Atha maggena hato samūlako.

388p_19Th2_472Ingālakuyāva ujjhito,
Visapattoriva aggito kato;
Napi naṁ passāmi kīriso,
Atha maggena hato samūlako.

389p_19Th2_473Yassā siyā apaccavek­khitaṁ,
Satthā vā anupāsito siyā;
Tvaṁ tādisikaṁ palobhaya,
Jānantiṁ so imaṁ vihaññasi.

390p_19Th2_474Mayhañhi akkuṭṭhavandite,
Sukhadukkhe ca satī upaṭṭhitā;
Sankhatamasubhanti jāniya,
Sabbattheva mano na limpati.

391p_19Th2_475Sāhaṁ sugatassa sāvikā,
Maggaṭṭhangikayānayāyinī;
Uddhaṭasallā anāsavā,
Suññāgāragatā ramāmahaṁ.

392p_19Th2_476Diṭṭhā hi mayā sucittitā,
Sombhā dārukapillakāni vā;
Tantīhi ca khīlakehi ca,
Vinibaddhā vividhaṁ panaccakā.

393p_19Th2_477Tamhuddhaṭe tantikhīlake,
Vissaṭṭhe vikale parikrite;
Na vindeyya khaṇḍaso kate,
Kimhi tattha manaṁ nivesaye.

394p_19Th2_478Tathūpamā dehakāni maṁ,
Tehi dhammehi vinā na vattanti;
Dhammehi vinā na vattati,
Kimhi tattha manaṁ nivesaye.

395 p_19Th2_479Yathā haritālena makkhitaṁ,
Addasa cittikaṁ bhittiyā kataṁ;
Tamhi te viparītadassanaṁ,
Saññā mānusikā niratthikā.

396p_19Th2_480Māyaṁ viya aggato kataṁ,
Supinanteva suvaṇṇapādapaṁ;
Upagacchasi andha rittakaṁ,
Janamajjheriva rupparūpakaṁ.

397p_19Th2_481Vaṭṭaniriva koṭarohitā,
Majjhe pubbuḷakā saassukā;
Pīḷakoḷikā cettha jāyati,
Vividhā cakkhuvidhā ca piṇḍitā".

398p_19Th2_482Uppāṭiya cārudassanā,
Na ca pajjittha asangamānasā;
"Handa te cakkhuṁ harassu taṁ",
Tassa narassa adāsi tāvade.

399p_19Th2_483Tassa ca viramāsi tāvade,
Rāgo tattha khamāpayī ca naṁ;
"Sotthi siyā brahmacārinī,
Na puno edisakaṁ bhavissati".

400p_19Th2_484"Āsādiya edisaṁ janaṁ,
Aggiṁ pajjalitaṁva lingiya;
Gaṇhiya āsīvisaṁ viya,
Api nu sotthi siyā khamehi no".

401p_19Th2_485Muttā ca tato sā bhikkhunī,
Agamī buddhavarassa santikaṁ;
Passiya varapuññalakkhaṇaṁ,
Cakkhu āsi yathā purāṇakanti.
