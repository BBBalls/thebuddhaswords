Sutta Nipata Chapter 4: The Chapter of the Eights - Aṭṭhakavagga

4:15 Violent Conduct

  • Translated from the Pali by Laurence Khantipalo Mills
    (More copyright information)

4:15 Violent Conduct

935Fear’s born assuming forcefulness—
see how the people fight!
I’ll tell you how I’m deeply moved,
how I have felt so stirred.

936Seeing how people flounder
as fish in little water
attacking one the other
its fearfulness appeared.

937Once I wished a place to stay,
but all the world is essenceless,
turmoil in every quarter,
I saw no place secure.

938Folks’ never-ending enmity
I saw, took no delight,
but then I saw the hard-to-see,
the dart within the heart.

939Affected by this dart
one runs in all directions
but with the dart pulled out
one neither runs nor sinks.

940On this, the training’s chanted thus:
Whatever bonds within the world
they should not be pursued
knowing in depth all sense-desires
for Nirvāṇa train.

941Truthful and not arrogant,
deceit none, slander, hate,
rid of greed’s evil, avarice
beyond them all’s the sage.

942Not sleepy, drowsy, slothful not,
living not with negligence,
taking no stand on arrogance:
that mind inclines to Nibbāna.

943Be not into lying led,
for forms have no affection,
know thoroughly conceit,
violence avoid fare thus.

944Delight not in the past,
nor be content with newness,
sad not with disappearance,
nor crave for the attractive.

945Greed I say’s “the great flood",
its torrent the rush of lust,
lust’s objects an imagining,
the swamp of lust is hard to cross.

946The sage on firm ground stands,
not swayed from truth, a paragon,
having relinquished All,
“peaceful" that one’s called.

947The wise indeed, all wisdom won,
on dharma not dependant,
wanders perfected in this world,
and envies none herein.

948Who sense-desires has crossed beyond,
undone worldly ties
and bondless, cut across the stream,
no longer grieves or broods.

949Let what’s “before" just wither up,
“after" for you be not a thing,
if then “between" you will not grasp,
You will fare at peace.

950For whom with mind-and-bodily forms
there is no “making-mine" at all,
grieves not when they are not,
and suffers here no loss.

951For whom there is no “this is mine"
nor no “To others it belongs",
in whom “myself" cannot be found,
Grieves not that “I have none".

952Asked upon one unshakeable,
I tell of this one’s goodness:
Not harsh, not covetous at all,
Steadfast, impartial everywhere.

953For one who’s steadfast, Knows,
That one does not accumulate,
Unattached to making effort,
Sees security everywhere.

954A sage speaks not as though
’Mong equal, low or high,
Serene, devoid of avarice,
Does not accept or reject.

Attadaṇḍasutta

935"Attadaṇḍā bhayaṁ jātaṁ,
janaṁ passatha medhagaṁ;
Saṁvegaṁ kittayissāmi,
yathā saṁvijitaṁ mayā.

936 Phandamānaṁ pajaṁ disvā,
macche appodake yathā;
Aññamaññehi byāruddhe,
disvā maṁ bhayamāvisi.

937 Samantamasāro loko,
disā sabbā sameritā;
Icchaṁ bhavanamattano,
nāddasāsiṁ anositaṁ.

938 Osāne tveva byāruddhe,
Disvā me aratī ahu;
Athettha sallamaddakkhiṁ,
Duddasaṁ hadayanissitaṁ.

939 Yena sallena otiṇṇo,
disā sabbā vidhāvati;
Tameva sallamabbuyha,
na dhāvati na sīdati.

940 Tattha sikkhānugīyanti,
yāni loke gadhitāni;
Na tesu pasuto siyā,
nibbijjha sabbaso kāme;
Sikkhe nibbānamattano.

941 Sacco siyā appagabbho,
amāyo rittapesuṇo;
Akkodhano lobhapāpaṁ,
vevicchaṁ vitare muni.

942 Niddaṁ tandiṁ sahe thīnaṁ,
pamādena na saṁvase;
Atimāne na tiṭṭheyya,
nibbānamanaso naro.

943 Mosavajje na nīyetha,
rūpe snehaṁ na kubbaye;
Mānañca parijāneyya,
sāhasā virato care.

944 Purāṇaṁ nābhinandeyya,
Nave khantiṁ na kubbaye;
Hiyyamāne na soceyya,
Ākāsaṁ na sito siyā.

945 Gedhaṁ brūmi mahoghoti,
Ājavaṁ brūmi jappanaṁ;
Ārammaṇaṁ pakappanaṁ,
Kāmapanko duraccayo.

946 Saccā avokkamma muni,
Thale tiṭṭhati brāhmaṇo;
Sabbaṁ so paṭinissajja,
Sa ve santoti vuccati.

947 Sa ve vidvā sa vedagū,
Ñatvā dhammaṁ anissito;
Sammā so loke iriyāno,
Na pihetīdha kassaci.

948 Yodha kāme accatari,
Sangaṁ loke duraccayaṁ;
Na so socati nājjheti,
Chinnasoto abandhano.

949 Yaṁ pubbe taṁ visosehi,
Pacchā te māhu kiñcanaṁ;
Majjhe ce no gahessasi,
Upasanto carissasi.

950 Sabbaso nāmarūpasmiṁ,
Yassa natthi mamāyitaṁ;
Asatā ca na socati,
Sa ve loke na jīyati.

951 Yassa natthi idaṁ meti,
Paresaṁ vāpi kiñcanaṁ;
Mamattaṁ so asaṁvindaṁ,
Natthi meti na socati.

952 Aniṭṭhurī ananugiddho,
Anejo sabbadhī samo;
Tamānisaṁsaṁ pabrūmi,
Pucchito avikampinaṁ.

953 Anejassa vijānato,
Natthi kāci nisankhati;
Virato so viyārabbhā,
Khemaṁ passati sabbadhi.

954 Na samesu na omesu,
Na ussesu vadate muni;
Santo so vītamaccharo,
Nādeti na nirassatī"ti.

Attadaṇḍasuttaṁ pannarasamaṁ.
